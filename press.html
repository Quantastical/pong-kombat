Description

Back in 1994, Stefan Gagne and his cohorts created Pong Kombat--a parody video game and, what would eventually become, a series--featuring lovable characters ahem paddles that fought to the death in epic battles of Pong.

The initial game was a school project, adding elements of Mortal Kombat to a Pong match, but it's simple and fun gameplay caused it to "go viral," quickly spawning off sequels, notoriously titled Pong Kombat 2 and Pong Kombat 3.

Now, after two decades, White Paddle returns to the web in...Pong...Kombat...4? How 'bout, Pong Kombat XX? Or, Pong Kombat HD? PongKombat.com? Well, whatever it is called, PONG KOMBAT is back. Which paddle will you choose?

History

Pong Kombat was originally developed by Stefan Gagne in 1994 as a school project. The purpose of this project is to follow the same principals that were employed in their initial release: learn something new and have fun doing it.

Like most people born in and around the 1980's, Jeff (Quantastical.com) grew up enjoying video games and, at an early age, became interested in programming. Naturally, he explored and experimented with source code in QBASIC games like NIBBLES.BAT and GORILLA.BAT and even made some text-based adventures from scratch.

Although never getting into the game industry, it has always been a passion, and a remake of Pong Kombat has been an interest since the early 2000s. It has remained a pet project, something that's been used to learn new languages, such as Java, Objective-C, or other 3rd party frameworks.

As a web developer, I start with a blank canvas and try to tell someone else's story as vividly as I can. Pong Kombat is a great story that seems to have faded over the years. I want to ensure that it's legacy lives on, to give interested developers (and myself) an example to learn from, and to honor some of my favorite video game franchises.

- Jeff Jenkins

Now, after nearly five years of practice, the project is finally being realized, two decades after Gagne's original.
